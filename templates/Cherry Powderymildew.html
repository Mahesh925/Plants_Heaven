Plant Disease Information: Cherry Powdery Mildew

Disease Scientific Name:

Podosphaera clandestina

Disease Name:

Cherry Powdery Mildew

Disease Description:

Cherry Powdery Mildew is a fungal disease that affects cherry trees, including sour cherry varieties. This disease is characterized by the development of a white, powdery substance on the leaves, fruit, and sometimes stems of cherry trees. It can lead to reduced fruit quality and yield if left untreated. Understanding the symptoms, transmission, prevention, and management of Cherry Powdery Mildew is crucial for cherry growers.

Disease Symptoms:

  • Cherry Powdery Mildew exhibits the following symptoms:
  • White Powdery Growth: The most noticeable symptom is the appearance of a white, powdery growth on the surfaces of leaves, fruit, and stems. This growth can make the affected parts look as if they've been dusted with flour.
  • Leaf Curling and Distortion: Infected leaves may exhibit curling, distortion, and yellowing. Severe infections can lead to premature leaf drop.
  • Fruit Damage: On cherries, the disease can cause fruit distortion, reduced fruit size, and a decline in fruit quality.
  • Symptoms typically appear during the growing season, especially in warm and dry conditions.

Disease Transmission:

Cherry Powdery Mildew spreads through airborne spores. Common transmission routes include: Wind Dissemination: Wind carries fungal spores from infected trees to healthy ones, facilitating the spread of the disease within orchards. Overwintering: The fungus may overwinter in dormant buds and resume growth in the spring.

Disease Prevention:

  • Preventing Cherry Powdery Mildew involves several strategies:
  • Resistant Varieties: Consider planting cherry tree varieties that are less susceptible to Powdery Mildew.
  • Pruning: Proper pruning to improve air circulation can reduce the humidity that favors fungal growth.
  • Fungicides: Apply fungicides as a preventive measure, especially during periods of high disease risk.
  • Sanitation: Remove and destroy infected plant material to reduce the disease's source of inoculum.

Disease Management:

  • Effective management of Cherry Powdery Mildew includes:
  • Fungicide Applications: In commercial orchards, fungicides are commonly used to manage the disease. Apply fungicides according to a recommended schedule.
  • Regular Monitoring: Regularly inspect cherry trees for signs of Powdery Mildew and initiate control measures as needed.
  • Pruning: Proper pruning to open up the canopy and improve air circulation can help reduce disease pressure.

By following these guidelines for prevention and management, you can reduce the impact of Cherry Powdery Mildew on your plants and ensure a healthierharvest.
