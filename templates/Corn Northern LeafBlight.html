Plant Disease Information: Corn (Maize) - Northern Leaf Blight

Disease Scientific Name:

Setosphaeria turcica

Disease Name:

Corn (Maize) - Northern Leaf Blight

Disease Description:

Corn (Maize) - Northern Leaf Blight, caused by the fungus Exserohilum turcicum, is a significant disease affecting corn crops. This fungal pathogen can lead to reduced yield and quality of corn. Understanding the characteristics, symptoms, transmission methods, prevention strategies, and management techniques for Northern Leaf Blight is crucial for corn growers.

Disease Symptoms:

  • Leaf Lesions: The most noticeable symptom is the development of elongated, cigar-shaped lesions on the leaves. These lesions are grayish-green to tan and have a distinct rectangular shape with parallel sides.
  • Lesion Development: Lesions may vary in size but tend to expand over time, merging into larger, irregularly shaped areas. As they progress, they turn brown, and the center may become gray or tan.
  • Reduced Photosynthesis: Severe infections can lead to reduced photosynthesis, affecting the plant's overall health and reducing grain fill.
  • Disease Progression: In humid and warm conditions, the disease can spread rapidly, causing significant damage to corn crops.

Disease Transmission:

Northern Leaf Blight spreads primarily through spores produced on infected corn residue and carried by wind or rain to healthy plants. Common transmission routes include: Wind dispersal of fungal spores. Rain splash, which can dislodge spores from infected debris and transfer them to healthy plants. Infected residue in the soil can serve as a source of inoculum for subsequent crops.

Disease Prevention:

  • Preventing and managing Northern Leaf Blight involves several strategies:
  • Crop rotation to reduce the buildup of inoculum in the soil.
  • Planting resistant corn varieties.
  • Timely application of fungicides, especially during periods of high disease pressure.
  • Removing and destroying infected crop debris after harvest.
  • Proper spacing and balanced fertilization to promote plant health.

Disease Management:

  • Effective management of Northern Leaf Blight includes:
  • Regular scouting and monitoring of corn fields.
  • Implementing fungicide applications as needed based on disease severity and weather conditions.
  • Adjusting planting density and timing to reduce humidity and create less favorable conditions for disease development.
  • Utilizing cultural practices like residue management and sanitation.

By following these guidelines for prevention and management, you can reduce the impact of Corn (Maize) - Northern Leaf Blight on your plants and ensure a healthier harvest.
